Muscles are the biological engines powering every human action — from heartbeats to voluntary movement. There are over 600 muscles in the human body, comprising 40% of total body weight.

SECTION 04 — MECHANISM

MUSCLE CONTRACTION
MECHANISM

⟶
SLIDING FILAMENT THEORY
Actin and myosin filaments slide past each other, shortening the sarcomere and contracting the muscle fiber in a ratchet-like mechanism.
⟶
ATP-POWERED CROSS-BRIDGES
Myosin heads bind to actin, power-stroke, detach, and reset using ATP — cycling 5–50 times per second at full effort.
⟶
CALCIUM ION TRIGGER
Ca²⁺ released from the sarcoplasmic reticulum exposes binding sites on actin filaments, initiating the entire contraction cascade.
